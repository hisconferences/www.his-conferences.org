HIS 2012 Call For Demos
HIS 2012: The 1st International Conference on Health Information Science
8th-9th, April, 2012, Beijing, China
http://e-research.csm.vu.edu.au/files/his2012/his.html

The International Conference on Health Information Science aims to integrate computer science/information technology with health sciences & services, embracing information science research coupled with topics related to the modelling, design, development, integration, and management of health information systems. Researchers, practitioners and developers are invited to submit demo proposals to HIS 2012. The topics include, but not restricted to, the following:
    •     Web related technologies/applications/Prototypes on Health Systems
    •     Telemedicine
    •     Web-based or Mobile Phone-based Medical Devices/Prototypes
    •     Health/Personal Information Systems
    •     Data Warehousing, OLAP, and ETL Tools
    •     Stream/Sensor Data Management
    •     User Interfaces and Data Visualization
Each accepted demo is required to have a poster and demonstrate during the demo sessions. HIS 2012 will provide space and standard equipment (PC and Internet connection) at the conference venue.
Demo Paper Submission
All demonstration submissions must be no more than 4 pages (including all figures) in LNCS format (http://www.springer.com/computer/lncs?SGWID=0-164-6-793341-0) and should provide a short and clear description about the system to be demonstrated, covering a summary of its novel characteristics, the list of its functions and features. Any submission that does not follow the guidelines will be rejected without review. The selection criteria used for demonstration proposals include novelty, technical quality, and presentation quality of the demonstrated system. Demo proposals need to be submitted via email to Chaoyi.pang@csiro.au as a PDF attachment before the deadline. Submissions should use Demo Proposal HIS 2012 as the subject line. For every accepted demo paper, HIS 2012 does require at least one person to register and present.
